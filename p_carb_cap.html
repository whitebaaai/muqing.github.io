Personal Carbon Capture Device

Storing CO2 via Olivine Mineralizations

Thirteenth International Conference on Climate Change: Impacts & Responses

Carbon capture and storage technology have long been utilized on an industrial scale. To provide the individual agency in addressing climate change, carbon capture technology is speculated for use on a personal scale. laking inspiration from the pioneer of the nirst-invented computer, we named the device "MarK-I" to evoke the possibilities or incorporating carbon-capturing technology into our daily routines within a design space at a personal scale.

Mark- employs mineral carbonation with olivine (MgSIO4) as the carbon-capturing technology to sequester and track carbon dioxide produced in a home environment. The device incorporated a researched-and-designed mechanism that accelerates the mineral carbonation process, actively creating data visualizations and instructions to guide and communicate with users

The design considers the afterlife of olivine filters. It proposes to occupy the existing recycling infrastructures to maximize the use of olivine based on its material capacity. The design of the material afterlife also includes an awarding system based on the blockchain system. It generates carbon coins in exchange for every batch of successfully reused carbon filters of olivine that is full from sequestering carbon dioxide as raw material for new industrial productions.

Elemental Analysis via SEM Imaging

We have collected all the available samples in different forms including, gel, rock, and commercial sand. We then performed the elemental Analysis (SDS/SEM) on the chemical compositions, and the surface comparisons on smoothness and area to ensure the samples are olivine at the nano-scale.

MVP Development plan for CO2-capturing Mechanism

REACTOR MECHANISM: DOUBLE PASS CO2 CAPTURE

The double-air-pass system has increased dwell time for air in the reactor tubes to maximize the contact with olivine particles.

- A basic single-pass system of olivine reactor tubes connected in-series with an input terminal at one end on the right, and an output terminal at the other end on the left.

- One CO2 monitors, each at the input and the output terminal.

- A slow-rate after CO2 monitor at the input terminal to increase the rate of air passing through the tubes

- A fast-rate air pump in parallel with the olivine tube system to recirculate the same air flow

Working prototype demo

The demo setup consists of a reactor tube of 1/16’’ olivine particles, with two valves located at each end for calibration purposes. One CO2-monitor both input and output terminals. A pipe with valve in parallel connection with the reactor tube for calibration processes.

State 1: The setup was calibrated initially by bypassing air through the olivine tube to ensure initial readings and offsets from both sensors are the same.

State 2: is when it is actively working. We have repeated calibrations multiple times during the process to eliminate bias.

Result:

Remove 1~2 ppm C02 per flow through.

The output data is as expected to be lower than the input data graph, which means the CO2 concentration in the air is being reduced.

The demo setup was tested in a fairly active classroom with dynamic flow of people, giving inconsistent captured CO2 concentrations in the airflow. This is reflected on the changing peaks on the graphs (windows, people coming in out around the device etc)

We have also performance the same test on the MVP plan (the double-pass system presented previously) and the resulted absorbance rate is 4x the result from this single pass demo setup.
