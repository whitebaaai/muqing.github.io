Responsive Bio-Sensing

Interpreting fungal languages through protein expressions

The project speculates on the construction of a genetic circuit based on bio-communications. The circuit consists of an environmental sensing analog circuit that captures electrical frequencies from mycelium colonizations as a signa switch to contro orofein exoressions or genetically engineered bacrerias.

The construction of an analog-to-genetic circuit captures electrical signals from mycelium colonizations over dufferent waste streams and uses such signal inputs to test and improve other mycelium growth used for bio-remediations or contaminated landscapes

As a proof of concept, the experiment was performed using a voltage generator instead of harvested mycelium signals to probe into an engineered voltage-sensitive fluorescent protein pJMK001, that contains PROPS genes in a pBAD TOPO backbone based on green-absorbing proteorhodops to express its fluorescent readout of membrane potential in E.coli

The analog-to-genetic circuit • captures electrical activities (reference: highest amplitudes: 0.2 and 0.3 m) from the fully colonized mycelium part of the brown oyster mushrooms uses the signals as a trigger input to control the fluorescence properties in PrOPS genes in the genetic circuit, which a synthetically engineered fungal protein would potentially replace.

a. Adjusting Gain

**Gain can be adjusted using R the potentiometer while setting R6 = R7 and fixing R5** - When R the potentiometer is 10kΩ: Av **= (-R7/R5)(1 + 2R/R) = -1 (1 + 2x30k/10k) = 7** - When R the potentiometer is 0kΩ: **G = Infinite**

Very High input indepance; Gain can be adjusted with only 1 resistor

b. Calibration Process

*Calibration Methodology:* - Input 5v into a voltage divider to generate milli-volts to the differential amplifier biased at 2.5v. - At each mill-volt point, adjust the R value; 0mv, **1mv (40Ω)**, 2mv **(80Ω)**, 3mv**(120Ω)**, 4mv**(160Ω)**, 5mv**(200Ω).** - Record each output voltage and plot the function curve. - Readjust the collected data value according to the function curve.

Reflections

Technical Considerations

- Replacing Retinal with Retinol.

- The voltage input can be a short impulse instead of a steady input.

- The voltage input can be varied beyond or below the standard 400V used.

Ethical Considerations

The project purposes an interactive sensing device that helps users better engage and understand their contributions to environmental restorations using biomaterials.

- Since the project manipulates an organic matter, there needs to be policies created around what natural conditions should be allowed for Myco-restorations within what conditions of the built environment should the cultivations of mycelium be allowed for industrial usage.

- The limitations on quantities, material sourcing considerations for engineering the plasmid used to generate signaling effect.

- Who and how could access the data collected from both residential and commercial users.

Further Goals

- Creating cultural engagement with local communities involved in mycelium cultivation.

- Encouraging organic formations of decentralized and user-oriented platforms for sharing data and monitoring processes.
