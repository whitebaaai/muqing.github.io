The Deployable Domesticity

Modular living systems of prefabricated kit of parts

In Silicon Valley, it is not that EVERY Sq m matters, but EVERY Sq mm. Not every young professional has enough living spaces, so to make the most efficient use of space, 360° of the volume needs to be useful.

With references to informal settlements, such as “the Burning Man”, and homeless encampments at “The Jungle”, the project explores distinctive proximities and patterns among mixed groups living in micro spaces. This body of study was applied to tackle the housing shortages in Silicon Valley with soaring housing prices.

Design interventions focus on the space-saving logic, which relies on a rotatable system of modified tetrahedrons to provide the maximum spatial use within the minimum spatial footprint. As a deployable system of the prefabricated kit of parts, the system can be planned to occupy both horizontal and vertical landscapes. The project also explores the materializations of prefabrications with technologies of CNC and 3D-printing and interlocking designs of self-henge frames and magnetic panels. .

Studies of Informal Settlements

"The Jungle” in the Silicon Valley, San Jose, CA

On December 11, 2016, I spent a day at “The Jungle”, where I witnessed the complex communities even after the 2014 evictions. The Jungle is located at Coyote Creek’s border in San Jose, covering over 65 acres of land. According to my on-site and media interviews, most Jungle settlers make a living by recycling materials at nearby shops. They also use leftover materials to amend and extend the tents they live. This experience set the start of the project to imagine a future landscape of Silicon Valley with affordable living systems for both young tech workers and the homeless.
