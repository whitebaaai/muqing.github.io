Gastro I/O

Making Gut Health More Accessible with In-vivo Sensing

During this 6-week design challenge, we were prompted to create a mobile app powered by AI and wearable tech to monitor and boost well-being and to receive instant feedback on both physical and mental health, making holistic wellness easily accessible.

With research efforts and brainstormed ideations, we investigated into colon cancer that is currently on the rise in young adults. The American Cancer Society reported 20% of diagnosis in 2019 were in patients under 55 years old–double the rate in 1995.

The design proposal arose from these researched findings that it is less widely known of the role of gut health in the development of colon cancer, and that there are not enough resources for people to easily learn and manage gut health.



Iterate UX Design Challenge, Mar.05 - Apr.25 2024

Role: Product Design, Scientific Research, UI/UX

Advisors: Padmashree Shetye, Jordan Hutchison

Collaborators: Oscar Chu, Kristine Nodalo, Ophelia Chen, Eric Garcia

Problem Statement

How might we...

"Develop an intuitive and user-centric Gut Health Application that gives individuals direct access to personalized educational content, advice and guidelines related to gut health for better managing and tracking of their day-to-day dietary habits, gut-related symptoms and overall healthcare progress?”

Key Reflections

Gut health plays a significant role and could be manifested in both our physical and mental health. Gastro I/O leverages real-time data from the body to generate precise data to fine-tune the LLM-based chatbot, which aims to intuitively provide highly personalized feedback to users. 

While introducing an intrusive way of data sensing, it is crucial to curate the experience with an in-depth understanding of the user’s preferences and considerations of the associated policies. It is also important to draw the line of making the application advisory-based vs. diagnostic-driven. 

In terms of improving the existing platform, our next priority step is to create an interactive chatbot for users to validate and refine the user experience of the app. The in-vivo sensor could remain optional for users who would compromise with less accurate feedback based only upon data from the food scanners and manual inputs.
