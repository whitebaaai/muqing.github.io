Diet I/O

Just-in-time nudge for changes in dietary decision-making

Diet I/O leverages real-time health data and AI-based image processing models to create users with just-in-time reflections for behavioral change.

It is a perceptually “misleading” strategy that encourages users to make informed decisions based on predicted consequences of accumulated behavioral patterns on the well-being of the users and their environments.

Given the ability to foresee possible futures based on the activities that people are often unconscious of, users are expected to pause and re-evaluate potential damages to their health and the places they belong to.

Achieving user reactions is a critical measure of success for educating the larger population about the symbiotic relationship between humans and the environment.

Overall Framework

The reflective nudge is a disruptive element between the phase of the user’s current and subsequent actions. The disruption consists of the user’s future appearance and the environment where the user resides under the associated impacts from collectively committed behavioral patterns of consumption. As the user proactively inputs basic health information about body conditions, the system generates a foreground of the predicted simulation based on calculations from glucose data from the user’s bloodstream. The background simulations are then created by analyzing the environmental impact of the user’s recorded food ingredients when committed collectively.
